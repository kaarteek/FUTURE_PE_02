Prompt Engineering Documentation

How I Built This with AI

Every piece of content above was generated using structured prompts. Here's the full prompt log — exactly what I asked Claude and why it worked.

Ad Type Prompt I Used Technique Why It Worked
Instagram Reel Hook "Act as a UGC creator for Deccan Bowl, a tiffin service in Hyderabad. Write a 30-second Instagram Reel script targeting IT professionals using the Hook → Problem → Solution → CTA format. Use a relatable office scenario." Role Assignment + Format Constraint Giving Claude a persona (UGC creator) + strict format produced a focused, platform-native script rather than generic marketing copy.
Facebook Ad Copy "Write a Facebook ad for Deccan Bowl targeting working mothers in Hyderabad aged 28–40. Headline should address a pain point. Body should build trust. End with a clear offer and CTA. Tone: warm, friendly." Target Persona + Tone Instruction Specifying the exact audience persona forced the output to be specific and emotionally resonant rather than broad and generic.
YouTube Shorts Script "Write a 30-second YouTube Shorts script for Deccan Bowl in UGC testimonial style. Include a 5-second visual hook, a first-person story from a subscriber, and a soft CTA. Add visual direction in brackets." Format Mirroring + Visual Scripting Asking for bracket-formatted visual directions made the output production-ready, not just copy.
Instagram Captions (8) "Generate 8 Instagram captions for Deccan Bowl tiffin service — 2 emotional, 2 fun, 2 behind-the-scenes, 2 value-focused. Include relevant hashtags. Keep tone consistent with a warm, homely brand voice." Batch Generation + Category Tagging Asking for captions by category in one prompt ensured variety while maintaining brand voice consistency.
Email Welcome "Write a welcome email for new Deccan Bowl subscribers. Tone: warm and personal, like a letter from the founder. Include what they'll receive, what to expect on Day 1, and a friendly sign-off." Founder Voice Persona + Expectation Setting The "founder letter" persona made the email feel human and personal rather than corporate.
WhatsApp Blast "Write a WhatsApp broadcast message for Deccan Bowl promoting a 50% first-week offer. Keep it under 120 words. Use bold text markers, emojis, and a bullet-point menu preview. End with an unsubscribe line." Length Constraint + Format Rules Strict word limit and formatting rules (bold with *, bullets) made the output instantly WhatsApp-ready without editing.
