About This Task

In this task, I used AI to create a complete **UGC (User Generated Content) Ad Pack** for Deccan Bowl — a Hyderabad home tiffin delivery brand. UGC ads are short, authentic-sounding content pieces that feel like real people recommending a product rather than a brand promoting itself.

**Content Created:** Hooks · Ad Scripts · Platform Captions · Hook Variations

## 🧠 PROMPTS USED

---

### Prompt 1 — Instagram Reel Hooks

**What I wanted:**
Five short, punchy opening lines that stop someone from scrolling past a reel in the first 3 seconds.

**Prompt I wrote:**
> "You are a UGC content creator for social media. Write 5 scroll-stopping hook lines for Instagram Reels for 'Deccan Bowl' — a Hyderabad home tiffin delivery service. Each hook must grab attention within the first 3 seconds. Target audience: working professionals and students aged 18–30 in Hyderabad who eat outside food daily. Tone: real, conversational, and relatable — like a friend talking, not a brand advertising."

**What I learned:**
- Saying "like a friend talking" stops the AI from writing in a formal, ad-like tone
- Mentioning the platform (Instagram Reels) helps the AI understand the format and length
- Specifying "first 3 seconds" pushes the AI to write hooks that are short and immediate

---

### Prompt 2 — Problem–Solution–CTA Ad Scripts

**What I wanted:**
Three full ad scripts — one for a working professional, one for a college student, and one for a new parent — each following the Problem → Solution → CTA structure.

**Prompt I wrote:**
> "Write 3 UGC-style ad scripts for 'Deccan Bowl' Hyderabad tiffin delivery using the Problem–Solution–CTA format. Each script should be 60–90 words and feel natural when spoken. Write one script for: (1) a working professional, (2) a college student, (3) a new parent. Each script needs a hook, a problem, a solution, and a strong call to action. Tone: warm, relatable, and honest — like a genuine recommendation from someone who uses the service."

**What I learned:**
- Three different audience personas = three completely different scripts, all for the same product
- Giving a word count (60–90 words) keeps scripts short enough for Reels and YouTube Shorts
- "Feel natural when spoken" is a great instruction — it prevents stiff, unnatural writing

---

### Prompt 3 — Multi-Platform Captions

**What I wanted:**
Ready-to-post captions for Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube — each platform has a different style and audience expectation.

**Prompt I wrote:**
> "Write social media captions for 'Deccan Bowl' Hyderabad tiffin delivery for 3 platforms: Instagram (casual, emoji-heavy, under 150 characters), Facebook (friendly and informative, 2–3 sentences), and YouTube Shorts description (SEO-friendly with keywords: Hyderabad tiffin delivery, home-cooked food Hyderabad, tiffin subscription Hyderabad). Write 2 captions per platform. Add relevant hashtags at the end."

**What I learned:**
- Platform-specific instructions produce very different outputs from the same AI
- Adding character limits prevents Instagram captions from becoming too long
- Including SEO keywords in the YouTube prompt makes the description searchable on Google

---

### Prompt 4 — Hook Variations for A/B Testing

**What I wanted:**
Six different hook types for the same product — useful for testing which style performs best.

**Prompt I wrote:**
> "Write 6 different hook styles for 'Deccan Bowl' Hyderabad home tiffin delivery. Use these exact hook types: (1) Curiosity hook, (2) Pain point hook, (3) Controversial statement hook, (4) Relatable moment hook, (5) Transformation hook, (6) Number or statistic hook. Keep every hook under 15 words. Target audience: Hyderabad working professionals aged 22–35."

**What I learned:**
- Naming specific hook frameworks (curiosity, pain point, etc.) forces creative variety
- A 15-word limit ensures every hook is punchy and usable directly in a video
- This kind of output is useful for A/B testing — running two versions to see which gets more views

## 💡 KEY PROMPT ENGINEERING LESSONS

**1. Write for one person, not everyone**
The best UGC scripts speak directly to one specific person — a student, a professional, a parent. This makes the content feel personal and real.

**2. Tone instructions change everything**
"Like a friend recommending" vs "professional marketing copy" gives completely different outputs. Always describe the tone you want in simple words.

**3. Platform matters**
Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube each have different audiences and content styles. Always mention the platform in your prompt.

**4. Word limits improve quality**
Without a word limit, AI tends to write too much. Giving a specific count (60–90 words, under 15 words) forces tighter, more usable output.

**5. Hook frameworks are powerful**
Learning named hook types (curiosity, pain point, transformation) and asking the AI to use them gives you a variety of options to test — not just one generic angle.
